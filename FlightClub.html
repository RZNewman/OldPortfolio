Flight Club

Make a fighter game that is both intuitive to play and provides interesting counterplay throughout the user experience.

Overview:

Flight Club is a fighting game that allows users to compete against each other locally. It is also a platformer game with several levels.

Initial:

>

We decided that a platform fighter would best serve a sandbox fighting game, using our experience of popular titles like Super Smash Bros. Melee and Rivals of Aether. While retaining the familiar, positive aspects of those games, we created a unique variant.

Work:

>

Using the Unity game engine, we built complex FSMs to handle the player physics and animations. We built a variety of attacks and maps and tuned them to provide a more developed, interactive experience.

Product:

Our game combines movement systems from platformers and attack types familiar to sandbox fighters in order to create a skill-based fighter game. The dash system creates a faster pace, while removal of complex techniques reduced the entry barrier.

How it works:

Player movement is dependant on the state of their character. Inputs and other influences, such as being hit or falling off an edge, cause state transitions.

The animatior FSM for Flight Club. Most transitions come from the 'Any State' node because the animatior follows our player scripts
