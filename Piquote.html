Piquote

Update a web application that takes images and quotes from a web article and makes them into a single photo for social media.

Overview:

Piquote prompts the user for a url of a web article. It finds the best quotes and pictures from the page by parsing the html. It gives the user a choice of picture and quote combinations, then outputs a single image of their selection.

Initial:

While the initial project was functional, it only provided the absolute basics. Sentences were retrieved by a very inconsistent tokenizer, and their quality was determined purely by the amount of punctuation. Images were not retrieved at all, and the composite images at the end were only html displays, not actual image files.

>

Work:

Using python, we built a system that grades selected sentences based on keywords and punctuation and takes relevant images from the site.

>

Product:

Our project allows users to select one of a combination of quotes and pictures, and receive an image corresponding to their choice. Our quotes have fewer punctuation flaws and are similar to quotes that humans might select while reading the article.

Focus:

Our project centered around higher quality sentence and image selection. I focused on improving the quality of the results delivered, while the other members worked on the design and how to measure the desired results. The team updated the presentation of the website, the prioritization of the quotes, and the way in which the quotes were graded.

A sample output screen from our final iteration

A sample of the word repository used for grading sentances

Solution:

The grading is done through a compiled database of keywords. The presence of these words is one of the factors used to weight the sentences.

I wrote code to completely separate sentences and make a list of proper nouns (based of occurrences of capital letter). It also stitches quotes together that span multiple sentences. Images are gathered from the sites by filtering out ones whose parent containers have instances of a select few classes or ids.

Future:

Due to constraints of time and technology, we were unable to create some of the features we wanted to. The next step would be creating a tweet for the user with the picture automatically. The quote selection could also be further refined with more keywords or a more nuanced approach.
